What is the event?

The 2016 U.S. Election has America’s attention. We have an opportunity to create voting solutions.

Introducing Hacking Voter Turnout, a 48-hour Futurethon designed to kickstart sustainable and long-lasting solutions to California’s Voter Turnout problem. The winning team’s solution could be messaging, strategy, products, campaigns, utilities, art, or technology, as long as it takes an innovative and aggressive approach to solving voter turnout. After all, a problem of this magnitude requires a whole community of cooperation to find a solution.

How it Works

Hear from Experts

The Futurethon will feature professionals with valuable insight on the problem at hand.

Team Up

Student and professionals choose teams based on their skillsets.

Propose & Prototype

Teams work to solve one of 1 of the 3 challenges.

Present

At the end of the 48 hour concepting period, teams present their idea to a panel of judges.

Rewards

The top three ideas, according to the judges, will be recognized and rewarded for their innovative thinking.

Fellowship

One group will be given a summer fellowship with USC to develop and implement their idea by October 2016.

The Challenges

Teams will choose 1 of 3 challenges

Online Voter Registration

Introduced in California in 2008 and used by 30 States, online voter registration has increased voting participation by 30%. How do we sustain that number for future elections?

Millennial Turnout

Many issues and limited convenience contribute to a low Millennial Turnout. How, by addressing those issues or circumventing them, do we increase Millennial Turnout by 50%? How do we increase convenience day-of voting for millennials?

Non-Presidential Election Engagement

Non-Presidential Elections have half as many Voters as Presidential Elections. How do we capture millennial interest on local politics and increase Midterm Election Turnout by 20%?
